OVERVIEW
--------
Comparison of ternary (base-3) and binary (base-2) computer architectures.
Ternary uses "trits" instead of "bits" as the fundamental unit.

Balanced Ternary Encoding:
  -1  (Red,   1.55 um)
   0  (Green, 1.30 um)
  +1  (Blue,  1.00 um)

WHY TERNARY IS MORE EFFICIENT
-----------------------------
Radix Economy measures hardware cost for representing numbers:

  Radix Economy = (number of digits) × (base)

  For representing the same range of values:

  | System     | Digits | Base | Economy | Relative Cost |
  |------------|--------|------|---------|---------------|
  | Binary     | 32     | 2    | 64      | 1.06×         |
  | Ternary    | 21     | 3    | 63      | 1.00× (best)  |
  | Decimal    | 10     | 10   | 100     | 1.59×         |

  The mathematical optimum is base e ≈ 2.718
  Ternary (3) is closer to e than binary (2), making it more efficient!

  Practical benefit: Same computational range with fewer components.


HISTORICAL REFERENCE: SETUN COMPUTER (1958)
-------------------------------------------
The Soviet Setun was the only mass-produced ternary computer.

  Specifications:
  - Word size: 18 trits (6 trytes of 3 trits)
  - Range: -387,420,489 to +387,420,489
  - Memory: 162 words (later 6,561 words)
  - Speed: 4,500 operations/second
  - Advantages noted:
    * Simpler arithmetic circuits
    * Better error detection (3 states vs 2)
    * More natural signed arithmetic (no two's complement)

  Legacy:
  - ~50 machines built (1962-1965)
  - Used until 1980s in Soviet universities
  - Praised for reliability and simplicity


SUGGESTED ARCHITECTURES FOR OPTICAL COMPUTER
--------------------------------------------

1. PROOF OF CONCEPT (9 trits)
   - Values: 19,683
   - Range: -9,841 to +9,841
   - Chip size: ~500 × 1,800 um
   - Purpose: Demonstrate ternary optical computing works

2. SETUN-COMPATIBLE (18 trits)
   - Values: 387,420,489
   - Range: ±193.7 million
   - Chip size: ~500 × 3,600 um
   - Purpose: Match historical ternary standard

3. MODERN STANDARD (24 trits)
   - Values: 282,429,536,481
   - Range: ±141 billion
   - Chip size: ~500 × 4,800 um
   - Purpose: Exceed 32-bit binary capability

4. HIGH PERFORMANCE (36 trits)
   - Values: 1.5 × 10^17
   - Range: ±7.5 × 10^16
   - Chip size: ~500 × 7,200 um
   - Purpose: Scientific computing, high precision

ADDRESSING MODES
----------------
Ternary addressing naturally supports:

  - Negative addresses (for stack-relative addressing)
  - Zero-centered arrays (index from -N to +N)
  - Symmetric data structures

  Example: 9-trit address space
    Range: -9,841 to +9,841
    Can address: 19,683 memory locations
    Natural center: Address 0
